## Video Rendering

The hero video is handled **entirely by your Next.js frontend**, not by Sanity. You have two options wired up:

1. **Upload to Sanity** (`heroVideo` field) — serves the raw MP4/WebM via Sanity's CDN. Simple, but Sanity's file CDN doesn't do adaptive bitrate streaming or automatic transcoding. For a showreel, this means the full file downloads before smooth playback, which can be slow on mobile.

2. **External URL** (`heroVideoUrl` fallback) — renders in an `<iframe>`. This is where you'd paste a Vimeo/YouTube embed URL (e.g. `https://player.vimeo.com/video/...?background=1&autoplay=1&loop=1&muted=1`).

**Recommendation: Use Vimeo as the source.** Alan already has his reel on Vimeo (`vimeo.com/alanx/storybook`). Vimeo handles adaptive streaming, transcoding, and compression — far better quality at lower bandwidth than serving a raw MP4. However, the current `<iframe>` approach needs a small tweak to work well as a background video (Vimeo's `background=1` parameter kills controls and makes it autoplay/loop/mute). I'd suggest we also add a self-hosted MP4 option as a polished alternative later when Alan has a final cut ready, but Vimeo embed is the right starting point.

One thing to note: the iframe currently has no `object-cover` equivalent, so it won't fill the hero area perfectly. We should improve that. Want me to make that fix now, or after you've tested adding the content?
